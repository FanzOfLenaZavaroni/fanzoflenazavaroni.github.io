<h2 id="infobox1"><a href="#infobox1">My Comments On What I Have AS A Quote From The Article</a></h2>

<p>I only have this one line from the article. It shows how the newspaper used Lena Zavaroni’s name as a casual reference in its commentary. The line I have is:</p>

<p>“…the gist of the Derby chants centred on Walshie being fat, which seems a harsh indictment coming from anyone other than Lena Zavaroni.”</p>

<p>Even on its own, this shows the paper using her name as a familiar reference, which makes it a small example of how she appeared in popular culture.</p>

<h2 id="infobox2"><a href="#infobox2">3rd Party Source On The Match</a></h2>

<p>The match took place the previous day, on 28 February 1996, when Leicester City drew 0–0 with Derby County at Filbert Street. Steve Walsh (“Walshie”) was playing for Leicester, which means the chants mentioned in the article were directed at him by Derby supporters. These match details are confirmed by independent football records.</p>
