<h2 id="infobox1"><a href="#infobox1">My Comments On What I Have AS A Quote From The Article</a></h2>

<p>I only have this one line from the article. It shows how the newspaper used Lena Zavaroni’s name as a casual reference in its commentary. The line I have is:</p>
<p>Her first LP &#8212; including number written by Bjork when she was eight &#8212; Was an instant hit.</p>
<p>A year later, Iceland's Lena Zavaroni had turned punk princess &#8212; and swapped fame for notoriety.</p>

<h2 id="infobox1"><a href="#infobox1">Details Missing From The Sections Of The Quote</a></h2>
<p>Her first album was Björk (1977) and the track she composed when just 8 years old was called Jóhannes Kjarval for flute as a tribute to the icelandic artist, 'álfur út úr hól'.</p>
<p>allmusic.com given the release date for the album as December 1977 so given Björk was born 21 November 1965 that means she was 12 year old when the album was released. not 11 years old as claimed by allmusic.com.</p>
<p>As to the comment on Lena Zavaroni that is the press using Lena's name as a shorthand for a young child and nothing more.</p>
<p>The “punk princess” label is broad, and because punk, post‑punk, new wave, experimental, alternative, and similar genres blur into one another in how people describe them, referring to Björk as a “punk princess” isn’t surprising.</p>
