<h2 id="infobox1"><a href="#infobox1">Summary</a></h2>

<blockquote>
<p><strong>Galaxy label signs up with CBS</strong></p>

<p>Phil Solomon’s Galaxy label has moved its distribution from President to CBS, which will now handle the back catalogue and upcoming releases.</p>

<p>The first CBS release will be Lena Zavaroni’s single <a href="/discography/singles/1978-03-17-i-shouldve-listened-to-mama-uk">Should Have Listened To Mama</a>, followed by an <a href="/discography/studio-albums/1978-songs-are-such-good-things">album</a> and several TV appearances including her own special. More material is planned from other Galaxy artists, and CBS will also distribute the Ipi‑Tombi cast album.</p>

<p>Solomon expressed confidence in Danny Doyle’s growing success and said the move to CBS was based on long‑term artist development.</p>

<p>Plastic Fantastic Records has taken on UK distribution for the Venture reggae label, with several singles and an album scheduled for release.</p>

<p>Hawk Records has opened a London office, aiming to expand internationally under the name Hawk International.</p>
</blockquote>
