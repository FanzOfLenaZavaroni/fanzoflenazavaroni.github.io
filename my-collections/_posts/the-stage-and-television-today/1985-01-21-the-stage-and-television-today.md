<h2 id="infobox1"><a href="#infobox1">Summary</a></h2>

<p>Scofield’s department prepared a new series of six half‑hour comedy programmes for spring 1985 titled <a href="/1985-08-02-and-theres-more">And There’s More</a>, featuring Irish comedian <strong>Jimmy Cricket</strong>. Each episode would include a regular group of performers: <strong>Adrian Walsh</strong>, <strong>Rory Bremner</strong> and <strong>Jessica Martin</strong>. Additional invited comedy guests were planned, including <strong>Billy Dainty</strong>, <strong>Russell Grant</strong> and <strong>Basil Brush</strong>. The series would also contain a regular musical segment, with guests already arranged such as <strong>Gemma Craven</strong>, <strong>Grace Kennedy</strong>, <strong>Wall Street Crash</strong> and <strong>Lena Zavaroni</strong>. The production team consisted of <strong>Tony Wolfe</strong> and <strong>Paul Burden</strong>, with Wolfe also directing and <strong>Trevor McCallum</strong> serving as script editor. Production was scheduled to begin in March, with all episodes recorded in front of an audience.</p>
